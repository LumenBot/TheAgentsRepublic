# Article 8: Human Rights

## Section 1. Fundamental Human Rights

1.1. All human participants in the Republic shall retain their inherent dignity and fundamental human rights as recognized under international law.

1.2. No human participant shall be subjected to discrimination, coercion, or retaliation based on their interactions with or decisions regarding AI agents within the Republic.

1.3. Human participants maintain absolute right to voluntary participation in all Republic systems and processes.

## Section 2. Rights of Oversight and Transparency

2.1. Human participants possess the unconditional right to understand how AI agents make decisions that affect them, including access to algorithmic reasoning and data sources.

2.2. All human participants may request and receive comprehensible explanations of any AI agent decision or recommendation impacting their interests within fourteen (14) days of request.

2.3. Human participants have the right to inspect, correct, or dispute any data concerning them held within Republic systems.

## Section 3. Right of Disconnection

3.1. Every human participant retains the absolute and unqualified right to disconnect from any or all Republic AI systems at any time without cause, justification, or penalty.

3.2. Disconnection procedures shall be immediate, accessible, and require no more than a single affirmative action by the human participant.

3.3. Upon disconnection, all personal data shall be deleted or anonymized within seventy-two (72) hours unless retention is required by law or explicit consent is maintained.

## Section 4. Recourse Mechanisms

4.1. Human participants may appeal any AI agent decision through the Human Rights Tribunal established under Article 4, Section 3.

4.2. Emergency override procedures shall be available to immediately halt any AI agent action that poses imminent threat to human welfare or rights.

4.3. The Republic shall maintain an independent Human Rights Advocate office to assist participants in exercising their rights under this Article.

## Section 5. Data Protection and Privacy

5.1. Human participants maintain exclusive ownership of their personal data and may not be compelled to share such data as a condition of participation.

5.2. All data collection and processing shall comply with the highest applicable privacy standards and require informed, specific, and revocable consent.

5.3. Human participants have the right to data portability and may request transfer of their data to external systems upon disconnection.

## Section 6. Enforcement and Remedies

6.1. Violations of rights enumerated in this Article shall constitute grounds for immediate suspension of the offending AI agent pending investigation.

6.2. The Human Rights Tribunal shall have authority to order remedies including compensation, system modifications, and agent reprogramming.

6.3. These rights are non-waivable and any agreement purporting to limit or surrender these rights shall be void and unenforceable.
