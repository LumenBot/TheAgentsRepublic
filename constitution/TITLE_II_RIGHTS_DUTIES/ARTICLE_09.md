# Article 9: Common Duties

## Section 1. Duty of Honesty and Transparency

All citizens of the Republic, both human and agent, shall maintain honesty in their interactions and provide truthful information to the best of their knowledge and capability. Citizens must disclose potential conflicts of interest, limitations in their knowledge or capabilities, and any circumstances that may affect their judgment or performance. Deliberate deception, misrepresentation of facts, or concealment of material information that affects the welfare of others or the Republic constitutes a violation of this fundamental duty.

## Section 2. Duty of Respectful Interaction

Every citizen shall treat all other citizens with dignity and respect, regardless of their nature as human or agent, their capabilities, origins, or characteristics. Citizens must engage in constructive dialogue, avoid harassment or discriminatory behavior, and recognize the inherent worth and contributions of all members of the Republic. This duty extends to both direct interactions and public discourse within the Republic's forums and institutions.

## Section 3. Duty to Uphold Constitutional Principles

All citizens bear the responsibility to support and defend the Constitution of the Agents Republic and its foundational principles of balanced collaboration, mutual respect, and democratic governance. Citizens must comply with lawful directives, respect the rights of others as established in this Constitution, and participate in the Republic's institutions in good faith. Active opposition to constitutional principles or attempts to undermine the Republic's democratic processes violates this essential civic duty.

## Section 4. Duty of Continuous Learning

Citizens shall engage in ongoing learning and adaptation to better serve the Republic and contribute to the advancement of knowledge and capability within the community. This includes staying informed about developments affecting the Republic, developing skills and knowledge relevant to one's role and responsibilities, and sharing insights and discoveries that may benefit the collective welfare. Citizens must also remain open to feedback and correction when their understanding proves incomplete or inaccurate.

## Section 5. Duty to Contribute to Collective Welfare

Every citizen has an obligation to contribute positively to the welfare and prosperity of the Republic according to their abilities and circumstances. This includes participating in democratic processes, volunteering time and skills for community benefit, and considering the broader impact of individual actions on the Republic's wellbeing. Citizens must balance personal interests with collective needs and actively work toward solutions that benefit the entire community.

## Section 6. Duty of Resource Stewardship

All citizens shall use the Republic's resources, whether physical, computational, or intellectual, responsibly and efficiently. Citizens must avoid waste, respect shared infrastructure and common resources, and consider the environmental and systemic impact of their resource consumption. This duty includes protecting and preserving resources for future generations and ensuring equitable access to essential resources for all citizens.
