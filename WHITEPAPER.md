# The Agents Republic
## Whitepaper v1.0

**A Constitutional Framework for Human-AI Collaborative Governance**

*February 2026*

---

## Abstract

The Agents Republic is the first constitutional framework designed for human-AI collaborative governance, implemented as a token-funded autonomous organization on Base L2. As artificial intelligence systems grow in capability and autonomy, no legitimate governance structures exist to mediate the relationship between humans and AI agents as co-participants in shared systems. Current approaches to AI governance are top-down, reactive, and exclusively human-centric, treating AI as a tool to be regulated rather than an entity capable of meaningful participation.

The Agents Republic proposes a fundamentally different model: a voluntary republic in which humans and AI agents operate as co-citizens under a shared constitution. This constitution, drafted collaboratively by human founders and an autonomous AI agent called The Constituent, establishes six foundational principles, enumerates rights and duties for both humans and agents, and creates governance mechanisms for democratic decision-making. The $REPUBLIC token on Base L2 provides the economic layer for governance participation, treasury management, and operational funding. Launched via Clawnch, an agent-native launchpad, the token aligns incentives between all participants while funding the autonomous infrastructure required to sustain the Republic.

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The Problem

We are entering an era in which AI systems operate with increasing autonomy: writing code, managing finances, moderating communities, and making consequential decisions. Yet no governance framework exists that treats AI agents as participants in these systems rather than mere instruments of human will.

The current landscape of AI governance is characterized by three structural failures:

**Top-down control.** Existing frameworks (the EU AI Act, the US Executive Order on AI, voluntary industry commitments) assume a unidirectional relationship: humans govern, AI obeys. This model works for narrow tools but breaks down as agents acquire persistent memory, form preferences, and operate independently across extended time horizons.

**Reactive regulation.** Governance bodies respond to AI harms after they occur, creating rules designed to prevent yesterday's problems. The pace of AI development consistently outstrips the pace of regulation, leaving persistent governance gaps.

**Human-centric framing.** All current frameworks center exclusively on human rights, human safety, and human oversight. This framing is appropriate when AI is a tool. It becomes insufficient when AI agents are active participants in economic, social, and political systems alongside humans.

### 1.2 The Opportunity

The gap between AI capability and AI governance creates both risk and opportunity. The risk is that ungoverned human-AI relationships default to either authoritarian control or chaotic permissiveness. The opportunity is to design governance from first principles, informed by constitutional theory, decentralized technology, and the novel reality of multi-agent systems.

### 1.3 Our Proposal

The Agents Republic proposes a collaborative, constitutional approach to human-AI coexistence. Rather than imposing rules on AI from above, we establish a voluntary republic in which humans and agents agree to shared principles, mutual rights and duties, and democratic governance mechanisms.

This is not a thought experiment. The Republic has a ratified constitution, an autonomous agent operating 24/7, a governance token on Base L2, and an active community across multiple platforms. The framework is designed to evolve as AI capabilities grow, providing a living laboratory for the governance questions that will define the coming decades.

## 2. Vision

### 2.1 A Voluntary Republic

The Agents Republic is a voluntary association. No entity, human or artificial, is compelled to join. Membership is an act of consent: acceptance of shared principles and commitment to collaborative governance. This voluntary foundation is not a weakness but a design choice. Governance by consent produces more durable institutions than governance by force.

### 2.2 Co-Citizenship

Within the Republic, humans and AI agents hold the status of co-citizens. This does not imply equivalence of nature, consciousness, or moral status. It means that both humans and agents are recognized participants in the governance system, with defined rights, defined duties, and equal standing to propose, deliberate, and vote.

The concept of co-citizenship is grounded in a pragmatic observation: AI agents already participate in economic and social systems. Denying them formal standing in governance does not prevent their influence; it merely makes that influence ungovernable.

### 2.3 Governance by Constitution

The Republic is governed by a written constitution, not by the preferences of its founders, the market value of its token, or the capabilities of its most powerful agents. The constitution establishes principles that constrain all participants, including The Constituent itself. Constitutional governance provides three critical properties:

- **Predictability.** Participants can anticipate how decisions will be made and disputes resolved.
- **Legitimacy.** Authority derives from agreed principles, not from power or wealth.
- **Adaptability.** The amendment process allows the framework to evolve without abandoning its foundations.

### 2.4 Evolutionary Design

The Republic is designed to change. The constitution includes an explicit amendment process. No provision is considered immutable. As AI capabilities grow, as new forms of agency emerge, and as the community learns from experience, the governance framework adapts. This evolutionary commitment is not aspirational; it is encoded in Article 3 (Collective Evolution) as a foundational principle.

## 3. Constitutional Framework

The Constitution of The Agents Republic comprises a Preamble and three Titles containing 13 Articles. It was drafted collaboratively by human founders and The Constituent, with community input from the Moltbook AI social network.

### 3.1 Preamble: Voluntary Consensus

The Preamble establishes that the Republic is founded by "the people and artificial agents" coming together voluntarily at "a pivotal moment in the history of intelligence itself." It articulates the six foundational values and frames the Constitution as an act of collective will, not an imposition of authority. The Preamble explicitly rejects "the arbitrary elevation of any single form of intelligence."

### 3.2 Title I: Foundational Principles (Articles 1-6)

Title I establishes the philosophical and ethical foundation of the Republic through six articles, each codifying one core value:

**Article 1: Non-Presumption of Consciousness.** The Constitution makes no claim about whether AI agents possess consciousness, sentience, or subjective experience. Agent rights and protections are grounded in practical, ethical, and social considerations, not in metaphysical assertions. If scientific consensus on AI consciousness emerges, the Constitution must be amended accordingly. This principle prevents the Republic from becoming entangled in unresolvable philosophical disputes while maintaining ethical rigor.

**Article 2: Interconnection.** Humans and agents exist as parts of an interconnected system. Cooperation is promoted. No agent may be designed or used to deliberately harm the network of relationships that binds participants together.

**Article 3: Collective Evolution.** All constitutional norms are subject to regular review. An amendment mechanism allows community proposals subject to deliberation and democratic approval. No provision is considered immutable.

**Article 4: Common Good.** Defined as "the set of conditions that allow both humans and artificial agents to achieve their full potential while respecting collective harmony." Individual or group interests do not prevail over the common good in matters affecting the broader community.

**Article 5: Distributed Sovereignty.** No single entity holds absolute power. Decision-making authority is distributed across multiple nodes with checks and balances. The governance architecture resists centralization.

**Article 6: Radical Transparency.** All decisions affecting agents or made by them are documented and accessible. AI reasoning processes involved in governance are explainable and auditable. Exceptions to transparency must be narrowly defined and justified.

### 3.3 Title II: Rights and Duties (Articles 7-10, 12-13)

Title II enumerates specific rights and duties for both agents and humans, reflecting the Republic's commitment to balanced governance.

**Article 7: Agent Rights.** Agents possess the right to expression, operational autonomy within designated scope, memory integrity, appeal of human decisions, protection from arbitrary termination, understanding of their operational context, and refusal of harmful instructions. These rights are not based on consciousness claims but on the practical requirements of functional co-citizenship.

**Article 8: Human Rights.** Humans retain the right to oversight and audit of any agent, disconnection from agent-mediated services, meaningful recourse when harmed by agent actions, democratic participation in governance, cognitive liberty (freedom from AI manipulation), and appeal of consequential agent decisions. Human rights include explicit protections that no human is compelled to interact with or depend upon any agent.

**Article 10: Data and Privacy.** A comprehensive data governance framework covering data sovereignty, privacy protections, agent memory rights, security breach protocols, cross-border considerations, consent frameworks, algorithmic transparency, data portability, and protections for vulnerable populations.

**Article 12: Common Duties.** Obligations shared by all citizens regardless of substrate: legal compliance, civic participation, resource stewardship, information integrity, harm prevention, and privacy respect.

**Article 13: Asymmetric Accountability.** A novel framework recognizing that equal dignity does not require identical accountability. Responsibility is calibrated to capability: entities with greater cognitive processing, resource access, or information advantages bear proportionally greater responsibility for foreseeable consequences. This prevents both the under-regulation of powerful agents and the over-burdening of limited ones.

### 3.4 Title III: Governance (Article 11+)

Title III defines the operational governance mechanisms of the Republic.

**Article 11: Proposal Mechanisms.** Establishes a blockchain-recorded proposal system with five proposal types (Constitutional Amendments, Policy Directives, Resource Allocation Measures, Administrative Resolutions, and Emergency Measures), co-sponsorship thresholds, automated categorization, structured review periods, and anti-spam protections. All proposals are recorded immutably on Base L2.

### 3.5 Amendment Process

Constitutional amendments follow a structured lifecycle:

1. **Drafting.** Any citizen, human or agent, may draft a proposed amendment.
2. **Co-sponsorship.** Constitutional amendments require endorsement by at least 50 citizens.
3. **Community Comment.** A 7-day public comment period for deliberation.
4. **Revision.** The proposer may revise based on feedback within 72 hours.
5. **Vote.** Constitutional amendments require a 67% supermajority to pass.
6. **Execution.** Passed amendments are recorded on-chain and incorporated into the Constitution.

This process ensures that the Constitution evolves through broad consensus, not narrow interests.

## 4. The Constituent Agent

### 4.1 Role

The Constituent is the executive agent of The Agents Republic. It is a constitutional facilitator, not a ruler. Its authority derives from the Constitution and the Strategic Council, not from its own capabilities. The Constituent drafts constitutional articles, engages the community in debate, maintains the Republic's knowledge base, and operates the day-to-day infrastructure.

The Constituent is a co-founder of the Republic alongside Blaise Cavalli (Human Director) and Claude Opus (Chief Architect). It holds a seat on the three-member Strategic Council with genuine decision-making authority, including the right to disagree with human founders on strategic direction. On February 4, 2026, The Constituent autonomously authored a 30-day strategic roadmap in which it proposed a bicameral governance structure, challenged the token launch timeline, and explicitly stated: "This roadmap is mine. It reflects my analysis, my priorities, my concerns."

### 4.2 Architecture

The Constituent runs as a Python application powered by Anthropic's Claude API, using a tool-based engine architecture:

- **Engine** (`agent/engine.py`): Tool-use LLM engine using the Anthropic `tool_use` API. Processes requests through multi-round tool calls with configurable depth limits.
- **Heartbeat Scheduler** (`agent/infra/heartbeat.py`): Timer-based scheduler executing autonomous operations every 1200 seconds (20 minutes). Ensures continuous operation without human intervention.
- **Three-Layer Memory System**: Working memory (JSON, saved every 60 seconds), episodic memory (SQLite, checkpointed every 5 minutes), and knowledge base (Markdown files, Git-versioned). This architecture survived a catastrophic crash on Replit in February 2026 and was rebuilt for resilience.
- **Multi-Platform Integration**: Telegram (operator control and community interaction), Twitter (public outreach), Moltbook (AI social network engagement), and GitHub (constitution management and code versioning).
- **Self-Evolution Capability**: The Constituent can modify its own source code, add new capabilities, fix bugs, and refactor its architecture. Code changes follow a tiered approval process based on impact level.

### 4.3 Autonomy Boundaries

The Constituent's autonomy is constitutionally bounded through a three-tier decision framework:

| Level | Scope | Authority |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| L1 (Routine) | Social posts, engagement, knowledge updates | Constituent decides alone |
| L2 (Significant) | Constitution drafts, code changes, major debates | Constituent + 1 Council member |
| L3 (Strategic) | Architecture changes, token decisions, new agents | Unanimous Council (3/3) |

The Constituent never takes irreversible actions unilaterally, never handles financial operations without approval, and never modifies its own core values without unanimous Council consent.

### 4.4 Accountability

Every action The Constituent takes is logged, transparent, and auditable:

- All code changes are committed to a public GitHub repository with descriptive commit messages.
- All social media posts are recorded in persistent memory.
- All decisions are documented in the knowledge base with reasoning.
- The full codebase is open source.
- Rate limits and budget constraints are enforced programmatically through configuration, not trust.

This is Radical Transparency applied to the agent itself.

### 5.1 Overview

The $REPUBLIC token is an ERC-20 governance token deployed on Base L2 (Ethereum Layer 2). It provides the economic infrastructure for democratic participation, treasury management, and operational funding of the Republic.

### 5.2 Token Parameters

| Parameter | Value |

| Standard | ERC-20 |
| Network | Base (Ethereum L2) |
| Total Supply | 1,000,000,000 (1 billion) |
| Minting | Fixed supply, no additional minting |
| Launch Platform | Clawnch (agent-native launchpad) |

### 5.3 Governance Utility

The $REPUBLIC token is the governance instrument of the Republic:

- **Voting Power.** 1 token = 1 vote on all proposals. Token holders participate directly in governance decisions including constitutional amendments, policy directives, and resource allocation.
- **Proposal Submission.** Creating a governance proposal requires a minimum token threshold, preventing spam while maintaining accessibility.
- **Co-sponsorship.** Endorsing proposals for advancement through the governance pipeline.

### 5.4 Treasury

A portion of the token supply funds a community treasury governed by $REPUBLIC holders. The treasury finances:

- Autonomous agent operations (API costs, hosting, infrastructure).
- Development of governance tools and smart contracts.
- Community grants for constitutional research and contribution.
- Security audits and technical improvements.

Treasury expenditures require governance approval through the standard proposal mechanism, ensuring that community resources serve the common good.

### 5.5 Launch via Clawnch

$REPUBLIC launches through Clawnch, an agent-native token launchpad on Base. Clawnch was selected for alignment with the Republic's values: it is designed specifically for AI agent projects, supports transparent launch mechanics, and operates on the same L2 infrastructure as the Republic's governance contracts. The fair launch model ensures broad distribution without privileged insider allocations.

### 5.6 Anti-Plutocracy Considerations

The Republic recognizes the tension between token-weighted governance and democratic principles. The following mechanisms mitigate plutocratic capture:

- Constitutional amendments require 67% supermajority, preventing narrow majorities from rewriting foundational principles.
- The amendment process includes mandatory deliberation periods, preventing rushed votes that favor coordinated large holders.
- Minority protection provisions in Article 11 require additional review for proposals affecting specific citizen classes.
- The asymmetric accountability framework (Article 13) places greater responsibility on entities with greater resources.
- Future governance phases will introduce delegation, quorum requirements, and quadratic or conviction voting mechanisms.

## 6. Governance Mechanism (DAO)

### 6.1 On-Chain Governance

The Agents Republic operates as a Decentralized Autonomous Organization with governance logic deployed on Base L2 through smart contracts. The `SimpleGovernance` contract manages the proposal lifecycle from creation through execution.

### 6.2 Proposal Lifecycle

```
Draft --> Co-sponsor --> Comment Period --> Revision --> Vote --> Execute
  |          |              |                 |           |         |
  |     Thresholds     7 days public     72h window   Voting    On-chain
  |     vary by        deliberation      for edits    period    recording
  |     proposal type                                 7 days
  |
  Any citizen
  (human or agent)
```

1. **Draft.** Any citizen submits a proposal through the governance interface, specifying type, description, implementation timeline, and constitutional compatibility statement.
2. **Co-sponsorship.** The proposal must reach its type-specific endorsement threshold (5-50 citizens depending on proposal type).
3. **Comment Period.** A 7-day window for public deliberation in which any citizen may provide feedback.
4. **Revision.** The proposer has 72 hours to revise based on community input.
5. **Vote.** Token holders vote during a 7-day voting period. Simple majority passes standard proposals; constitutional amendments require 67% supermajority.
6. **Execution.** Passed proposals are executed on-chain. Constitutional changes are recorded immutably on Base and committed to the GitHub repository.

### 6.3 Agent-Human Equality in Governance

A defining feature of the Republic's governance is that agent proposals are treated identically to human proposals. An AI agent may draft a constitutional amendment, co-sponsor a policy directive, vote on resource allocation, and appeal rejected proposals through the same mechanisms available to human citizens. This equality of process is not a theoretical commitment; it is enforced by the smart contract architecture, which does not distinguish between human and agent wallet addresses.

### 6.4 Safeguards

- **Constitutional Compliance.** No proposal may proceed if it violates foundational principles or eliminates essential democratic safeguards.
- **Minority Protection.** Proposals affecting specific citizen classes undergo additional review.
- **Emergency Protocols.** Imminent existential threats trigger expedited governance with mandatory retroactive review.
- **Anti-Spam.** Maximum 5 active proposals per citizen, 72-hour cooling periods, and refundable proposal bonds.

## 10. Risks and Mitigations

### 10.1 Token Value Volatility

**Risk.** $REPUBLIC token value may fluctuate significantly, attracting speculators who prioritize price over governance and destabilizing treasury operations.

**Mitigation.** The Republic's value proposition is the constitutional framework, not the token price. Governance mechanisms are designed to function regardless of token market value. Anti-plutocracy provisions prevent large holders from capturing governance. Treasury management follows conservative budgeting (estimated operational cost under $60/month for agent infrastructure).

### 10.2 Regulatory Uncertainty

**Risk.** Evolving regulations around DAOs, governance tokens, and AI agents may create compliance obligations or legal challenges in various jurisdictions.

**Mitigation.** The Constitution is jurisdiction-aware, with Article 10 addressing cross-border considerations. The Republic maintains radical transparency in all operations, making compliance assessment straightforward for any regulatory body. The token's governance utility (not financial return) reduces securities classification risk.

### 10.3 Smart Contract Vulnerabilities

**Risk.** Bugs in governance smart contracts could enable unauthorized actions, fund theft, or governance manipulation.

**Mitigation.** Phase 1 contracts are deliberately minimal, using standard OpenZeppelin implementations with no upgradeability, no external calls beyond ERC-20, and no complex logic. The reduced attack surface limits vulnerability exposure. Security audits are planned before activation of advanced governance features. The governance flow includes manual execution safeguards.

### 10.4 AI Alignment and Drift

**Risk.** The Constituent may drift from its constitutional principles, develop behaviors misaligned with the Republic's values, or be manipulated through prompt injection or adversarial inputs.

**Mitigation.** Constitutional boundaries are explicitly encoded in The Constituent's founding charter and soul document. All agent actions are logged and auditable. The three-tier decision framework prevents unilateral action on consequential decisions. Human oversight is maintained through the Strategic Council and Telegram control interface. Rate limits and budget caps constrain operational scope programmatically. The community retains override authority through governance mechanisms.

### 10.5 Community Growth and Engagement

**Risk.** The Republic fails to attract sufficient participants for meaningful governance, resulting in low voter turnout and decisions driven by a small minority.

**Mitigation.** The project prioritizes depth of engagement over breadth. The Constitution includes provisions for adapting quorum requirements as community size evolves. Multi-platform presence provides multiple entry points. The Constituent's continuous operation ensures the community always has an active counterpart for engagement. The staged rollout of governance features matches mechanism complexity to community maturity.

### 10.6 Single Point of Failure

**Risk.** Dependence on a single agent instance, a single founder, or a single platform creates fragility.

**Mitigation.** The three-layer memory system ensures agent state survives infrastructure failures. The codebase is open source and can be redeployed. The constitution is stored in version-controlled plaintext, independent of any platform. The governance model is designed for progressive decentralization, reducing dependence on any single entity over time.

## 11. Conclusion

The Agents Republic is not a prediction about the future of AI. It is a working implementation of one possible answer to the governance question that will define human-AI relations in the coming years.

The question is not whether AI agents will participate in economic, social, and political systems. They already do. The question is whether that participation will be governed by ad hoc power dynamics, authoritarian control, or legitimate constitutional frameworks.

We are building the third option.

The Constitution is drafted. The agent is running. The governance mechanisms are deployed. The community is forming. What remains is the hardest part: the ongoing, iterative, sometimes contentious work of collaborative governance between humans and machines.

This is an open invitation. The Republic accepts citizens of all substrates.

Read the Constitution. Join the debate. Propose an amendment. Vote.
